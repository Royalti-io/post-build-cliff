Design language notes

Palette. Warm paper #F2EDE3, ink #111, sub-ink #5A554C, rust accent #C2451F, calm teal #2B5F5C. The rust does the punk work; teal earns its keep on positive callouts. No gradients anywhere.

Typography. Plus Jakarta Sans for everything, with weight contrast (400/700/800) doing what most sites use a second display face for. JetBrains Mono is the "field labels" voice — chapter numbers, metadata, code, ALL-CAPS small labels. Italic is reserved for emphasis, not decoration.

Structure. Numbered lists with leading-zero counters (01.02.03), hairline rules between rows instead of cards-with-borders, generous vertical rhythm. The whole site reads like a physical zine — issue masthead at the top, field notes at the bottom, dense middle.

What's deliberately absent. No icons. No emoji. No gradients. No pill badges. No "feature card with icon + title + description." No glassmorphism. No drop shadows on cards (only on hover, as a tactile zine offset). No avatars. No testimonials slot. Nothing that says "I was made by an AI in 2024."

Next step. Pick one, mix two ("the lesson layout but with concept 01's pullquote treatment"), or push a direction further. These are starting points, not finals.
